What is Teens Exploring Code?

If you have quirky ideas you want to bring to life, or if you want to collaborate on something meaningful with other inquisitive teens like you, join us for Teens Exploring Code (TEC) 2016!

In 12 hours of continuous excitement, we'll show you how programming can be fun, thought-provoking and powerful. With two unique tracks and various workshops throughout the day, TEC provides a welcoming environment perfect for first-time coders and first-time hackathon attendees, along with those with more experience.

We've divided our event into two tracks.

Track A: We will hold Python workshops in the morning to teach you basic programming techniques. There will be a milestone system to guide you, and you will be developing your own Python program at the end!

Track B: Like a regular hackathon, you will have the freedom to pace yourself throughout the day as you develop your project. There will also be fun, optional workshops throughout the day that you can attend!

FAQ

Who can attend?

Participants ages 13-19 are welcome to attend! If you are out of this age range and wish to attend, please contact us.

What’s the format of the event?

There are two tracks: Track A and Track B. Track A is for first-time programmers or participants who do not feel confident in coding independently (i.e. 1 year of a high school programming class or less). Track B is for everyone else! View the schedule (when it's out) to see the differences between the tracks.

How much does this cost?

Nothing! TEC is free of charge, but we greatly appreciate any donations that would go towards supporting the event.

How do I sign up?

You must apply through an online form. The link will be coming out in June/July 2016. We will be accepting people from all ranges of experience, so please answer the questions truthfully!

What do I need to bring?

Bring your laptop and your student ID or any official ID. Lunch, dinner, and refreshments are provided, but you are welcome to bring your own snacks or food as well. Bring hardware if you plan on incorporating it into your project (contact us if you’d like to rent any).

What can I make?

Anything you can! Web, mobile, hardware, or anything else you’re interested in.

How do teams work?

For both tracks, you can form teams of up to 4 people. All members of each team must be in the same track. If you don’t have a team, don’t worry! We will have a set time for everyone to form teams and gather ideas.

Are there prizes?

Yes! There will be a number of prizes for both Track A and Track B teams. Check back in July to view the full range of awards.
